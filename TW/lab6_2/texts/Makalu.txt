Makalu[2] (Chinese: 馬卡魯峰; pinyin: Mǎkǎlǔ fēng; Nepali: मकालु हिमाल, romanized: Makālu himāl) is the fifth highest mountain in the world at 8,485 metres (27,838 ft). It is located in the Mahalangur Himalayas 19 km (12 mi) southeast of Mount Everest, on the border between Nepal and Tibet Autonomous Region, China. One of the eight-thousanders, Makalu is an isolated peak whose shape is a four-sided pyramid.
